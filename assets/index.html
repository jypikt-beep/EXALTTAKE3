Why Choose EXTRUSION ALTERNATIVES?

As our name implies, Extrusion Alternatives offers many material options to suit a wide array of tubing applications. We work closely with the leading PVC resin manufacturers like Alpha Gary, Colorite, and Teknor Apex to provide custom medical tubing made from innovative plasticizer formulations and the very latest medical compounds.

In addition to these DEHP-Free and Phthalate-Free selections, we offer many alternatives to PVC including: Polyurethane, Thermoplastic Elastomers, and various multi-layer PVC-free formulations.

These flexible products are complemented by a full offering of rigid medical thermoplastics like LDPE, HDPE, Polypropylene, and Nylon. All of these materials are virgin, non-regrind resins, from commercially available sources you know and trust. Our open market approach provides device manufacturers with the largest selection of materials to design to, a clear path to regulatory compliance, and a non-proprietary basis to build devices from.

While design options and material flexibility is our philosophy, it is our uncompromising, single-minded approach to quality that sets us apart. A QR-compliant system not only ensures the tightest in tolerance control, it also dictates that every inch of tubing is run through a pyrogen-controlled process, straight into the confines of a hepa-filtered clean environment for cutting, coiling, inspection, and packaging. From our facility to yours, the end result is better tubing, yielding better devices.

Our open-source material alternative, dedication to high quality products, and customer-centric commitment to service have built our loyal customer following. We welcome the opportunity to earn your business and stand ready to serve your needs.

Medical Grade TPU Tubing for Single Bore & Multi Lumen Catheters

Thermoplastic Urethane (TPU) is the most versatile of the TPE's, offering elasticity of rubber combined with the strength and processibility of thermoplastics. TPU can be either aromatic (better mechanical properties) or aliphatic (better chemical, ultraviolet & weather resistance) and are well suited for applications requiring flexibility or durability. This includes seals, gaskets, protective covers, and wheeled goods. Tubing made with TPU exhibits good cold temperature flexibility and chemical resistance, and offers the following features and benefits:

  • Weather & UV resistance
  • Good surface finish
  • Abrasion resistance
  • High tensile strength
  • Good resistance to chemicals and oils
  • Low temperature flexibility
  • Resistance to hydrolysis & microbes
  • Tear resistance

Tapered, Bump, & Funnel Medical Tubing for Drug Delivery Catheters, Cosmetic Surgery Applications, Enteral Feeding

Specialized computer controlled methods are used to manufacture dimensional changes along a length of tubing. The process can be used to create bump tubing, tapered tubing, and funnel tubing, with gradual changes in inner and outer diameters.

Tapering is often performed by heating and drawing a tube to lower its diameter size. Bump tubing can be manufactured by induction heating and expansion.

Tapered or bump tubing is used in a variety of medical device applications including:

  • Cosmetic Surgery Applications
  • Enteral Feeding
  • Surgical Catheters

DEHP Replacement Options

Recently I read an article in regards to DEHP tubing replacement materials. This is a fairly common concern and question on how to go about selecting alternative material.

First, the data on the effects of DEHP on health is not conclusive and many believe it is safer than DEHP replacement materials. Because of the high volatility of PVC resins, they combine well with both low and high temperature limit plasticizers. DEHP has long been used as a low temperature resistant plasticizer. In the manufacturing of medical tubing the ideal product should have high productivity with low heat build-up. The reasoning suggests DEHP is the best suited plasticizer in some cases.

However, because it has been found that DEHP can leach from PVC material under high temperatures, or in the case of medical devices and blood bags into blood products. The concern is possible damage to the liver, kidneys and lungs with prolonged exposure.
